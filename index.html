The Challenges.

Example: Muck Pile Detection using Deep Learning

Institute for Advanced Mining Technologies, RWTH Aachen University 🔗
Coding Machine Learning Sensors
The main task of an underground LHD loader is to move material from a blasted face to a crusher, orepass or truck. To fully automate the operation of an LHD loader, the loader must be able to detect the muck pile during its bucket filling operation. As the dusty and poorly lit underground environment poses a serious challenge, automating muck pile detection is one of the main obstacles towards fully automated operation. Your task is to build a solution using LIDAR, RGB-Cameras, Computer Vision and Machine Learning to detect the muck pile in order to automate the bucket filling operation of an LHD loader. You will be provided with all the necessary sensor modules, Nvidia GPU hardware to train neural nets and pre-labeled data. Join the Institute for Advanced Mining Technologies in its quest to solve LHD loader automation and help make underground mining more safe and environmentally friendly. People from any background and creative solutions welcome!

Digital Twin – From Edge to Cloud to Insight

Talpasolutions GmbH 🔗
Coding Analytics Communication
With IoT, Big Data, and modern AI, we can optimize processes and significantly lower maintenance costs – the biggest drivers in mining operation costs. We log raw machine data and then perform advanced analytics in our cloud. The key challenges here are almost oppositional requirements of edge and cloud computing. Your task is to combine data from different sources in the edge format MDF4, then send the data to an endpoint where you will convert it into the standard Big Data format Parquet. Your analysis of the data will lead to insights that optimize mining production and maintenance. You will have real machine data of a dump truck from a mine available for this task. Our challenge requires a broad skillset, so make sure your team has expertise in programming, quantitative analysis, and mechanical engineering. Join us in making the future of mining more efficient and sustainable with cutting-edge technology. We hope to see you work together in a diverse and interdisciplinary team, to come up with brilliant new ideas - just like we at talpasolutions do every day!

Track Drive Condition Monitoring of Mining Excavators

Komatsu Germany GmbH 🔗
Mechanical Analytics Machine Learning
It is crucial to know the wear status of mining excavator track drives, because that helps to avoid costly unplanned downtime. To improve the current situation of risky and time-consuming manual geometric measurements, a concept of an automatic wear monitoring has been developed, that aims for detecting the wear during regular operation. As a proof of concept, a few hundred events were measured on a 550t-excavator and manually analyzed. Depending on the operating conditions, evaluation of different physical effects visible in sensor data allows conclusions to the current wear of the track drive. The task is to design an automatic algorithm to estimates the wear by evaluating the course of signals. To achieve that, toolboxes from Matlab (Signal Processing, Neural Network, Statistical) are to be applied. The outcome of the approaches should be validated by the manual evaluation results. To get you on track, an overview of the physics, videos and prepared sensor data will be provided. Take up the challenge and help make mining safer & smarter. Everyone, in particular students from engineering and computer science, is welcome.

Smart Weighing - CAN-2-OPCUA

PFREUNDT GmbH 🔗
Coding Electronics Communication
A system is to be created that accepts the weight and status data of a platform scale and makes it available via an OPC UA server. The platform scale will be connected to a PFREUNDT WB60 electronic system. The WB60 puts the determined weight and status values on a CAN bus (J1939). This data is to be recorded on a PC and visualized using OPC UA.

PosSys 4.0 - 2D position detection system

Becker Mining Systems AG 🔗
Coding Electronics Sensors
A 2D positioning validation system should be developed for the purpose of evaluating the reliability and accuracy of a collision awareness and proximity detection module. Given the position of a mining vehicle and a known target, the distance between these two must be determined. An easy to use validation system with known precision is therefore required. Utilizing the same ranging technology as the collision awereness module, as well as using trilateration, a position from distances to known anchor points shall be determined by the validation system.

Your Organizers.

The Institute for Advanced Mining Technologies of RWTH Aachen University develops technologies for the automation and digitalization of mining machines and processes to enable safe, efficient and responsible raw material extraction. An important core area of the institute is the development of sensor technologies and corresponding algorithms for the generation, acquisition and processing of data and information along the entire data-information-value-chain. The challenging environmental conditions in mines place particularly high demands on people, machines, sensors and algorithms and require robust, connected and intelligent systems and solutions.
The VDMA represents over 3,200 mainly small and medium size member companies in the engineering industry, making it one of the largest and most important industrial associations in Europe. With an export quota amounting to 96 per cent, mining technology is one of the most export-oriented branches of the German engineering industry. VDMA Mining represents well-known, mainly medium-sized companies from the sectors open cast mining/materials handling, underground mining, mining processing technology and consulting, research and development. 145 companies merged in VDMA Mining representing more than 90 per cent of the entire trade volume.
